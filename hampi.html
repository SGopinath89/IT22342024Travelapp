Facts

Sacred Relic: The temple enshrines what is believed to be the left canine tooth of the Buddha, making it a pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.

Long History: Construction began in 1592, but the relic itself has a much longer history. Legends say it was brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD.

Royal Connection: For centuries, possession of the tooth relic was seen as legitimizing a king's rule. The temple was therefore built within the Royal Palace Complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy.

World Heritage Site: Recognized by UNESCO in 1988 for its cultural and religious significance, the Dalada Maligawa is a jewel in Sri Lanka's crown.

Unique Architecture: The temple complex showcases Kandyan architectural style, with influence from previous shrines that housed the relic.

Myths

Guardian Deity: One myth suggests the temple is guarded by a powerful deity called Kumara Bandara. This invisible guardian is said to protect the sacred tooth relic and bring misfortune to those who intend harm.

Indigestion Test: An interesting tale says that in the past, potential kings would undergo a strange test involving the relic. They were offered food before visiting the shrine, and if they suffered indigestion after paying their respects, it was believed they were unfit to rule.
