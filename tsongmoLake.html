Facts

** World's End is a sheer cliff with a staggering drop of about 4,00È¶Å (1,200 meters). It's one of the most visited parts of Horton Plains National Park and a major tourist attraction in Sri Lanka.

** The name might be dramatic, but World's End isn't actually the edge of the world. There's a smaller cliff about 1 km away with a 1,000-foot (300-meter) drop, known as Mini World's End.

** On a clear day, you can see the Indian Ocean, a distance of 81 kilometers to the south. The view from World's End encompasses cascading hills, lush greenery, and distant mountains, making it a scenic viewpoint.

** World's End is located within the Horton Plains National Park in the Nuwara Eliya District. The park itself is rich in biodiversity and home to various flora and fauna, including patana grasslands, cloud forests, and wildlife like sambur deer and leopards.

** The trek to World's End is a well-maintained path and a popular activity for tourists. It's a moderate hike with scenic views along the way.

Myths

The Ancient Fall: One legend speaks of a mighty king who ruled the central highlands. In a fit of rage, he threw his disobedient queen off the precipice, creating the sheer drop of World's End.

The Mythical Creatures: Another tale suggests that the strong winds howling around World's End are caused by massive creatures with wings so large they can block out the sun as they fly by.

The Lost Waterfall: Some believe a hidden waterfall once cascaded down the cliff face, creating a mesmerizing sight. This waterfall is said to have disappeared mysteriously.
